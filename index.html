About this week

This week I have provided some content on articulating design decision, posted some updates on additional activities available from the RGD for new grads who are entering industry in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic and given more details on the LinkedIn Networking week. This week's live session will be about 30 minutes in length and will go over these materials with time for discussion. Please note, this is our last class of the semester, and the remaining weeks will be used for 1:1 exit interviews.

  • Virtual Classroom
    Check back at 2pm on Friday
  • RGD Virtual Career Development Conference
    The RGD is planning a virtual Conference for grads with virtual presentations, virtual panel discussions and one-on-one virtual reviews to help them succeed in their future careers. We anticipate over 150 industry professionals will donate their time and expertise over the three days of activities as well as in a Slack Workspace for on-going discussions and Q&A. Called Creative Directions, the event is based on one you may recall we organized for many years in Toronto. Scheduled for April 27 and 29 and May 1, you can see the program taking shape at the link above. They will be emailing Student Members details and inviting them to register individually for the event.
  • RGD Student Awards
    As you may know RGD’s 2020 Student Awards will distribute $22,000 in cash prizes in recognition of student excellence. The deadline for entries is May 8. In addition to the promotion that our winners receive, we are inviting all entrants to let us know if they would like us to promote their entries across RGD communications channels in advance of judging. The goal is to promote most, if not all, of the work submitted (with some basic quality controls in place) across on our social media (reaching 30,000+), in our e-newsletter (sent to 24,000+) and on our website. Again we’ll be emailing Student Members about this but you may also want to spread the word to your faculty and students to help make them aware.
  • RGD Mentorship Program
    RGD is in the process of developing a roster of virtual Mentors to be available to assist grads. This will be based on our existing Mentorship Program for Provisional RGDs with RGD Student Members who are graduating this year having access to over 50 virtual RGD Mentors to choose from.
